Cambio

-- Objective --
Either rid yourself of all your cards or have the lowest point total hand after Cambio is called.

-- Rules --

- Start -
-> Each player gets dealt 4 cards arranged 2 x 2.
-> The remaining cards are stacked in the middle of the playing area.
-> Before the turns start, each player can look at the two cards closest to them once.

- Turn -
-> On your turn, you draw a card from the middle stack and look at it.
-> You can then choose to either play the card to the discard pile or swap this card
  with one of your cards on the table.
-> If you swap the card from the stack with one of your cards from the table, the card you
  swap with must go in the discard pile.
-> If you discard straight from the stack, the ability of the card is activated whereas if you
  swap the stack card with your own and discard the swapped card, it does not active the card's
  ability. (The ability of each card is listed below in the MAPPING section)
-> Once a card is discarded to the pile, your turn ends.
-> At the beginning of your turn, before picking any card from the middle deck, you may call
  Cambio. This ends your turn and allows all other players one remaining turn until the round
  is over.
-> You should only call Cambio if you believe that the value of your current cards is less
  than every other player and cannot be influenced heavily before the game ends.

- Sticking -
-> When a card is laid on the discard pile, anyone has the ability to stick a card on top.
-> If the value of the card matches the card placed on the discard pile, you have successfully
  stuck. If however the value of the card doesn't match, you have failed to stick.
-> You can choose to stick either one of your own cards or an opponents card.
(You should only try to stick if you are sure that you will successfully stick)
-> If you successfully stick a card, you will be able to lessen the number of your cards by one
  but the means of which depends on whether you stuck your own card or an opponent's.
-> If you had successfully stuck your own card, by nature, you will have rid yourself of one card.
-> If you had successfully stuck an opponent's card, you can return to the opponent one of your
  own cards.
-> If you fail to stick correctly, you will increase the number of your cards by one.
-> If you fail to stick your own card correctly, you must blindly pick up a card from the middle
  stack.
-> If you fail to stick an opponent's card correctly, you must return their card to them in the
  same position you took it from and then pick up a card from the middle stack.
-> Each player can only attempt one stick per turn and if someone successfully sticks, no one
  else can stick to that card until the next turn.
-> You can stick to the top of the discard pile anytime during a player's turn.

Clarifying Examples:
-> If you know an opponent has a 7 and the current top of the discard pile has a 7, you can stick
  their card to the discard pile as long as you manage to stick it before them.
-> If on your turn you pick up a 10 from the middle stack and know you have another 10, you can
  discard the 10 from the stack and try to immediately stick your card on top. Since you discarded
  from the deck, the ability of the 10 is activated as well.

-- Win Conditions --
-> If you are able to get rid of all yours cards first, from sticking, then you automatically win.
-> If you call Cambio, everyone has their last turn and you end up with the lowest valued set
  of cards on the table, you win.
-> If Cambio has been called but not by you, everyone has their last turn, and you have the
  lowest valued set of cards on the table, you win.
-> If you have the lowest valued set of cards and another player(s) has the same value, all
  players who did not call Cambio win whereas the person who did call Cambio loses.

Clarifying examples:
Consider 4 players A,B,C,D.

If player A calls Cambio and players B, C and D have their last turn. Here are a list of win outcomes with values. Values are arranged (A, B, C, D).

--- Value --- | --- Win --- 
 (2, 5, 6, 7) | (X, 0, 0, 0)
 (0, -1, 3, 8)| (0, X, 0, 0)
 (2, 2, 4, 5) | (0, X, 0, 0)
 (1, 1, 1, 3) | (0, X, X, 0)
 (4, 3, 0, 0) | (0, 0, X, X)

Ability Descriptions
Peek - Look at one of your own cards secretly
Spy - Look at an opponent's card secretly
Blind swap - Swap one of your cards with an opponent's without looking.
Peek, Spy and Potentially swap - Peek at your own card, Spy on an opponent's and then decide
  if you want to swap.
