King of Fish

-- Objective --
Be the first player to get rid of all their own cards.

-- Rules --

- Start -
-> Each player is given 7 cards.
-> The remaining cards are stacked in the middle of the playing area.
-> A random player then starts their turn.

- Turn -
-> Choose whether you wish to quiet/loud swap, lay a pair or pick up a card from the middle pile.
-> If you have a pair of cards (a pair of 7s or a pair of kings for example), you can lay them regardless if
  they are not the same colour.
-> If you choose to lay a pair or pick up a card from the pile, your turn is over and the next player commences
  their turn.
-> If you decide to swap, you can either soft swap with a chosen player or loud swap with a chosen player.
-> Quiet swap involves taking a card of a given player at random and handing them a card of your choosing
  back to them, except the card you just took.
-> Loud swap involves asking a player of your choosing if they have a specific card (like a 2 or a 9 for example).
  If they have that card, they must give it to you and you can return a card of your choosing back to them. If
  they do not have the card you ask for, you must pick up 2 cards from the pile and your turn is over.
-> It should be noted that regardless of whether you decide to swap a card or not, your turn must end with you
  either picking up a card from the pile, or laying down a pair.
-> Once you have completed this procedure, the turn is passed to the next player.

Clarifying Examples:
-> You can start your turn already with a pair, but choose to quiet/loud swap your other card. Even if the swap
  doesn't help you make another pair, you can then end your turn by laying down the pair you had initially.

-> If you loud swap for a card and are successful, and the card you take does not help you to make a pair, even
  though the move may be considered strange, you do not get a 2 card penalty. Though, if they cannot lay down a
  single pair after the swap, they will still have to pick up 1 card.

-- Win Condition --
The player who first lays down all their cards through laying down pairs wins the game. Since each card has a pair,
the game has a natural end and cannot go on indefinitely.
